Krishna Vachan
"The perspective of Life"

The Bhagwat Gita is a sacred text that teaches us how to live a meaningful and fulfilling life. It is a dialogue between Arjuna, a warrior prince, and Krishna, the Supreme Lord, who guides him through his doubts and dilemmas on the battlefield of Kurukshetra. The Bhagwat Gita offers various perspectives on life

Krishna Vachan
"The perspective of Dharma"

Arjuna learns that he must perform his duty as a warrior, even if it means fighting against his own relatives and friends. He must not be attached to the results of his actions, but act for the sake of the greater good. This is the essence of karma yoga, or the yoga of action.

Krishna Vachan
"The perspective of Knowledge"

Arjuna learns that he is not the body, but the eternal soul, which is part of the Supreme Soul, Krishna. He must realize his true identity and nature, and transcend the illusion of the material world. This is the essence of jnana yoga, or the yoga of knowledge.

Krishna Vachan
"The perspective of Bhakti"

Arjuna learns that he must surrender to Krishna, the supreme controller and benefactor of all living beings. He must love and serve him with all his heart, mind, and soul. This is the essence of bhakti yoga, or the yoga of devotion.
